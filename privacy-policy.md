# DeSpark — Privacy Policy

**Effective date:** June 12, 2026

This policy explains what data DeSpark collects and the choices you have. It is written in plain English on purpose — and because DeSpark collects nothing, it is short.

---

## 1. The short version

**DeSpark does not collect, store, or transmit any data about you.** The app works entirely on your device:

- No network access — the app never connects to the internet.
- No analytics, no crash reporting, no advertising, no tracking SDKs.
- No accounts, no sign-in, no identifiers.

Every image you clean is processed locally on your phone and never leaves it.

---

## 3. Data that stays on your device

Everything below is stored locally on your device, inside DeSpark's private app container. We never see it. If you have iCloud Backup enabled on your iPhone, Apple includes this data in your encrypted iCloud backup — that backup is between you and Apple; we have no access to it.

- **Images you open** — the photo you pick, paste, or share into the app, held in memory while it is processed.
- **Gallery history** — after a successful clean, DeSpark keeps the before/after pair and a small thumbnail so you can revisit and re-share results from the Gallery tab.

You can delete any of this at any time from within the app (delete a single result in its viewer, or Settings → "Clear gallery"). Uninstalling the app removes all of it.

Nothing is sent anywhere. There is no "our servers" — the app has no server.

---

## 4. Permissions the app asks for

- **Photo library (choosing an image)** — the system photo picker runs outside the app; DeSpark receives only the image you explicitly select, never your whole library.
- **Photo library (saving)** — iOS asks for add-only permission the first time you save a cleaned image. This permission lets DeSpark add images to your library; it does not let it read your library.
- **Clipboard** — read only when you tap "Paste image", via the standard iOS paste mechanism (iOS shows its own paste notice). DeSpark never reads the clipboard in the background.
- **Share Extension** — when you use "Share → DeSpark" from another app, the extension receives only the image you shared and hands it to the app through a private on-device container.

---

## 5. How the processing works

Watermark removal runs entirely on your device: the app detects the visible sparkle watermark in the image corner and reverses its blend. Only the **visible** watermark is affected — Google's invisible SynthID provenance signal embedded in Gemini images is not read, altered, or removed by DeSpark.

---

## 6. What we never do

- We never transmit your images or any derivative of them.
- We do not sell or share personal information (as those terms are defined under the CCPA/CPRA) — we have none to sell.
- We do not bundle any third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking SDK.
- We do not request App Tracking Transparency permission, because there is no tracking.

---

## 7. Children

DeSpark does not collect data from anyone, including children. The app has no accounts, no social features, and no advertising.
